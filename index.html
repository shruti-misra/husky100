Starting with Gratitude

My journey at the UW began in 2012, when I arrived in Seattle from Dubai to study Molecular Biology. I was 17, in a different country, nervous yet excited. Almost a decade later, I am an engineer, still abuzz with excitement. However, my nervousness has been replaced by a solid sense of identity and values, which have been cultivated through a vibrant mosaic of UW experiences.

The Husky experience has given me countless opportunities to learn, fail, grow and reflect on who I am and what is meaningful to me. Upon graduation, I will not only possess invaluable professional skills, but will have a wealth of incredible relationships for the rest of my life. The UW has shaped me into who I am now and I am grateful to be a part of its community and have the opportunities it has to offer.

Identities

In addition to identifying as a heterosexual woman of color, my Husky experiences have revealed the following facets of my identity:
  • Engineer
My education has made me a confident engineer who is comfortable navigating a highly technical field and addressing challenges pertinent to the world.
  • Educator
Through my experiences as an educator at the UW, I have recognized that empathy, evidence and community drive my teaching philosophy.
  • Experiential Scholar/Entrepreneur
UW's innovative ecosystem has encouraged my interests in innovation and entrepreneurship, which inspire my graduate research and future goals.
